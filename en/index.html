The saga

"A Mari Usque Ad Mare": not just a motto, but a route to follow.

Two seas, two lands, a destiny that spans centuries. From the shores of Europe to the waters of the New World, from African routes to the markets of the East, the saga follows the generations of a merchant family through three centuries of history: journeys, conquests, trade, fractures.

Each volume portrays an era and a different face of power: the blood of conquest, the silence of trafficking, the calculation of the great trading companies, the ideas that change the world. Not a celebration, but an honest account of how the modern world was born, and of its price.

Four novels are already published. The route is charted through to the tenth volume.

The novels

Volume I

From One Sea to Another

Two seas. Two lands. A destiny that spans centuries.

In the heart of an age of shifting borders and root-searching identities, a man embarks on a journey that will take him from the windswept shores of Europe to the mysterious waters of the New World.

Among ancient coats of arms, forgotten oaths and waves that hold secrets, the story of a journey that challenges history, nature and itself.

Volume II

Blood and Soil

Conquest is never born in silence. It is born of blood and the hunger for possession.

In the New World, the promises of the journey collide with a harsh reality shaped by contested lands, daily violence and fragile alliances. It is the moment when survival turns into domination, and land ceases to be a refuge to become a stake.

It is the time when roots are planted by force, and nothing can grow without bearing their mark.

Volume III

The Breath of Africa

The world expands, but the price grows heavier.

New routes open towards coasts where trade concerns not only goods, but human lives. History enters a shadowed zone where profit justifies every decision and distance dulls the weight of guilt, while individual conscience is tested by irreversible choices.

Not everything that breathes is free. And not everything that enriches can be forgotten.

Volume IV

The Indies and Silk

When violence grows silent, it takes the form of trade.

Routes multiply, empires stretch outward, and power learns to speak the language of elegance and wealth. Great trading companies turn oceans and ports into instruments of influence, between cultures that brush past one another without understanding and alliances built on advantage.

Silk shines, but it does not warm. And every route traced carries an invisible price.

The author

Armand Du Silence

Author of historical fiction. With the saga "A Mari Usque Ad Mare" he reconstructs three centuries of trade routes and family destinies, pairing the sweep of the adventure novel with the precision of historical reconstruction.

Published by Aurea Scripta Editions in Italian, English and French.
